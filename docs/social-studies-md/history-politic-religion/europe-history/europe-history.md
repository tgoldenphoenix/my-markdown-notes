# General Europe History

**Louis XIV** (Louis-Dieudonné; 5 September 1638 – 1 September 1715), also known as Louis the Great (Louis le Grand) or the **Sun King** (le Roi Soleil), was King of France from 1643 until his death in 1715. His verified reign of 72 years and 110 days is the longest of any sovereign.

The **Palace of Versailles** is a former royal residence commissioned by King Louis XIV located in Versailles, about 18 kilometres (11 mi) west of Paris, France.

## Other

[The House of Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Habsburg), also known as the House of Austria, was one of the most prominent and important dynasties in European history.

The [Congress of Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna) (Đại hội Viên) of **1814–1815** was a series of international diplomatic meetings to discuss and agree upon a possible new layout of the European political and constitutional order after the downfall of the French Emperor Napoleon Bonaparte.\
This Congress also shape Europe border after the collapsing of the Holy Roman Empire.



[Austro-Prussian rivalry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Prussian_rivalry): Austria and Prussia were the most powerful German states in the Holy Roman Empire by the 18th and 19th centuries and had engaged in a struggle for supremacy among smaller German kingdoms. The rivalry was characterized by major territorial conflicts and economic, cultural, and political aspects.

The [Hundred Days](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hundred_Days) (French: les Cent-Jours), also known as the War of the Seventh Coalition (French: Guerre de la Septième Coalition), marked the period between Napoleon's return from eleven months of exile on the island of Elba to Paris on 20 March 1815 and the second restoration of King Louis XVIII on 8 July 1815 (a period of 110 days).
